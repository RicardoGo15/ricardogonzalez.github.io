Senior Capstone Project:
Automotive Wheelchair Ramp

For my Senior Capstone Project we were placed in team of five people and worked with a sponsor. Our team's sponsor was a PhD graduate in Aerospace Engineering at UCSD who works as a Teacher Assistant. He was born with Spinal Muscular Atrophy. This limited his movement to only his hands and head. He uses a wheelchair and is transported around in a large, clunky minivan.

The task that our sponsor wanted help with was designing an ADA foldable ramp design that would attach to the chasis of a Tesla Model X. He was hoping to purchase a Tesla Model X to replace his large minivan. However, there was no existing ADA ramp that would allow his large wheelchair to enter the falcon doors of the Model X. We then worked on designing a foldable ramp attachment for the Model X that would take into account the dimensions of the door and wheelchair while being easy to manufacture.

For our final project deliverables, our team created a 50/50 foldable ramp model in SolidWorks. This showed our components as well as placement of the motor and the arm and slot mechanism that would lift the ramp. We provided our sponsor with a Build of Materials and the contact information of an equipment mobility specialist that could help manufacture this custom modified Tesla Model X.





Jacobs School Of Engineering
Robot Contest: FORTLIFT

I paricipated in a robot contest led by the Jacobs School of Engineering. This tested student's ability to design and prototype a fully functioning robot. The contest was held on a court which had a wall separating two teams. Each team had a bucket and 5 hacky sacks on their side. The goal was to create a robot that was capable of lifting and scoring hacky sacks into an opponents bucket in a 3 minute timeframe. My team consisted of three people. We conceptualize a design in SolidWorks, used failure mode and effect analysis in order to troubleshoot issues, and built our robot in order to compete.

We used a forklift design with a winch motor system using a string to lift the forks up and down the tower base. Our team ran a free body diagram analysis to know the torque that the motor required to output, and the weight of the forks to allow for smooth vertical motion. Our design was made entirely out of acryllic. We attached rubber bands onto the acryllic wheels and added a caster wheel to allow for turning mobility and control.

Our team competed in the contest and was able to score an average of 3 points against our five competitors. Although we did not win, I was proud of our robot because it taught me about the process of design: starting with brainstorming ideas as a group, going with a design and creating a prototype, troubleshooting issues that come up, and fabricating a working model.

Motorcycle Engine

Drawing 1: V-Twin Motorcycle Engine
Drawing 2: CamShaft Assembly with Valves and Piston
Drawing 3: FlyWheel
Drawing 4: Conrod

I began this motorcylce engine project to improve my skills with Solidworks and my knowledge on engines. The engine assembly is the first drawing. It has all the components of a good working V-twin engine. To begin, it has a camshaft with valves controlling the intake and release of gas in the pistons. The pistons are attached to conrods that convert the sliding movements of the pistons into a rotation movement. Finally, the flywheel takes this rotation movement and smooths out this energy. It then uses that energy through the chain to begin the compression stroke of the pistons, again allowing the engine to continue to run. Drawing 2,3,4 are detailed drawings of the components I explained with important dimensions marked.
